Houdini Master Program – Film & Game (DOOBOB)

Hello. I have worked on dozens of Hollywood films at studios including DNEG, ILM, Weta, and MPC. Until recently I was an Effects Supervisor at DNEG while also teaching at a university in Canada, and I am currently continuing my career as a Technical Art Director at a game company in Los Angeles.

This course is designed around 20+ years of production and teaching experience. It is built to help you gain the essential skills every FX artist needs: practical FX knowledge, how to structure a strong portfolio, and how to keep learning independently. 12 Modules

Curriculum

The course moves step by step from foundational Houdini FX concepts to portfolio-driven project work across twelve modules.

Module 01

Controlling particles the way you want

  • Houdini interface, reference gathering, particle fundamentals, POP
  • Handling fast-moving objects, solver fundamentals, collision workflow
  • Introduction to Newtonian physics

Module 02

Introduction to Math in Houdini

  • Core math functions you actually need
  • Vector addition, subtraction, multiplication
  • Dot product and cross product

Module 03

VEX

  • Building an infection solver
  • Using Attribute Wrangle effectively

Module 04

Disintegration Project

  • Emit particles
  • Growth effect using the infection solver
  • Vellum cloth

Module 05

RBD Destruction: Building Destruction I

  • Planning a destruction setup strategically
  • Fracturing, RBD Bullet Solver, constraints

Module 06

RBD Destruction: Building Destruction II

  • Secondary particles and volumes
  • Rendering

Module 07

Burning Character: Sticky Fire Advanced Technique

  • Smoke sourcing
  • Pyro simulation
  • Rendering

Module 08

Character-driven underwater effects

  • Essential elements for underwater scenes
  • Bubble and blood setup

Module 09

Procedural Effects for VFX and Games

  • Recreating a Frozen-style effect
  • Ground deformation effect study

Module 10

Zombie Burning and Zombie Explosion

  • Growth effect using the infection solver
  • Applied RBD workflow

Module 11

Recreating the Ground Interaction effect from Transformers

  • Understanding the core ground interaction formula
  • Using dot product and reflect
  • Expanding the setup into multiple variations

Module 12

Recreating the Venom transformation effect for VFX and games

  • Procedural trail setup
  • POP Fluid and VDB post process
  • UV transfer
